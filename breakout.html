Breakout Sessions

Join us for breakout sessions focusing on key topics lead by industry experts. Each session is geared towards acclimating you for real world skills and job preparation.

Friday, March 20, 2015
Morning Sessions: 11a - 12p



How to break into the creative world outside of college
Jordan Wollman, Chief Creative Office & Partner at Aria


In my decade plus of hiring, firing, and managing creative talent, it has always stood out to me how little of what we in advertising do for our clients, we do for ourselves. This session will explain the secret sauce to finding the exact job you want, and what things you're probably being told that will only make your resume (even a great, fancy, amazing one) another resume in a big old stack of resumes.

Jordan Wollman has, in one way or another, been involved in the creation of brands since his early years, where he spent his days watching his father design everything from corporate identities to brochures, early websites to communication architectures. Jordan has spent his entire professional career (starting at age 14) focusing on branding, brand development, and brand marketing. Jordan once owned a record store, a record label, helped develop a curriculum for a design school in Russia, wrote a book on After Effects, and was the youngest certified LightWave 3D trainer ever. A published author, international speaker, and multiple-time entrepreneur, Jordan's body of work and myriad interest is vast: design, history, politics, architecture, fine woodworking, and of course his loving family, are the obsessions that keep him going. Jordan is Chief Creative Officer and Partner at Aria, a tourism and economic development-focused brand agency.





Creating a More Collaborative Community to Enhance the College Experience
Jacob Cotton, Asst. Professor of Design at Coastal Carolina University


In a world where the competition is fierce and the final project may result in x-acto driven fatalities, a student must seek out every advantage to survive the harsh landscapes of the critique wall. One of the greatest advantages that a student has is every classmate in the room. Today, you’re a student; tomorrow, you’re a professional. Learn how to cultivate this creative advantage into a collaborative community of future designers.





Start your career. Grow your career (A story of success, failure and luck)
Lisa Goin


Embarking on your new career endeavors can be daunting and exciting all at the same time. Don't miss this story (25+ years in the making) of early learnings, hard earned success, seemingly devastating failure and plum dumb luck. A story about a creative mind, who started much where you are now, and pushed out into the world to work with some of the great creative minds of our time including, Saul Bass, David Carson, April Greiman, and Sean Adams. As well as early opportunities to work within the dream job of the music industry Capital Records, Sony Music, and Hans Zimmer. This session will prove to be inspiring, full of tips and tricks to market yourself as well as emphasizing key pillars for success. don't miss this educational and entertaining break out session. 



Afternoon Sessions: 1 - 2pm



Thinking Under Fire: Creative Logo Competition
Josh Ege


There will be a time in your career, more often than you might think, when you will be up against a tight deadline. During these times, quick thinking, concept development, and sketching are crucial. In this session, you will get the chance to test the speed of your conceptual skills. The sketches will be judged by the quality of the concept and the craftsmanship of the sketch. The winner will receive an inspiring identity inspired prize.

Over his 13-year career, Josh Ege has has the opportunity to work at advertising agencies, design boutiques, in-house design departments and as a freelance concept designer. Josh currently focuses on logo and poster design in his professional practice. He is also an Assistant Professor at Texas A&M University-Commerce, where he teaches classes that involve brand design, art direction, print collateral and interactive design.

His work has been recognized by Print Magazine, Communication Arts, Graphis, HOW International Design Awards, American Institute of Graphic Arts, American Graphic Design Awards, Art Directors Club of Houston, and the DSVC Professional Show.





"Designer" is the last thing you wanna be
Sung Kim


How do you design a product? Do you approach it from a designer's perspective or approach it as the voice of the product? How do you become a voice of the product when you have no expert knowledge in that field of study or industry, for example designing the menu for a sushi restaurant or an app that runs complex mathmatical algorithm. In reality, you know more than you think you do. You just have to be able to recognize the "patterns."

Who is Sung Kim?
Deemed 51st Best Designer in Technology in 2013 by Business Insider. Founder of Gestures, Inc. Currently UX/UI Consultant at Samsung. Designer for various startups, including Blownaway, Matter, Olé, and ModelRevolt. Lead designer for the Sam's Club Android app, Bud Light's first mobile site for touchscreen devices, Joinred.com in 2008, which was lauded by Bono. Optimized the 7-Eleven.com interior pages and restructured the Fedex Office Workstation login process that's currently being used nationwide.





How not to suck at presenting your work
Megan Mead


As a graphic design student, school is teaching you how to create smart, badass work that will hopefully get you hired upon graduating. What it isn’t teaching you, however, is how important it is to be good at presenting that work and how presentation skills can impact your career. In this breakout session you will learn the importance of giving your work the spotlight it deserves, and come away with an array of storytelling strategies and acting techniques that will help you improve your presentation performance. This will be a fun, hands on experience that will help you shake off some of those portfolio review jitters.

Megan Mead is a freelance art director, designer and speaker stationed in Austin, TX. She majored in communication design at the University of North Texas with a double minor in theatre and marketing, giving her a unique view point, straddling the world of performance and design. Trained to better understand vocal, breath and acting exercises, she is very interested in how theatrical training can supplement design education, and has spoken on this topic at several conferences including SXSW 2013, Rise Week Austin and Design Big Week AIGA Dallas. She is also very excited to be speaking at the HOW Design Live conference in May.

How Not To $@&% a Brand’s Story
Aaron Opsal


What does Dr. Seuss, Wes Anderson and Ground Hog Day have to do with selling just about everything, and saving your ass at the same time? A brand is a promise to always do right by your followers. Learn how to create that promise, story and brand vision. See how a little proper planning can lead to some pretty impressive designs. Analog Techniques in a Digital World
Kyle Steed


Let’s be honest, the “hand-made” movement is a big trend right now. From letters, to posters, to commercials. It’s everywhere. And what’s the one thing we can count on with trends? They will change. 

My name is Kyle Steed. I’m an artist, illustrator and professional doodler. I love working for myself and getting to create fun, unique pieces of work that inspire others. I take great honor in working with large clients on national campaigns as much as I do working with small clients in my own neighborhood. It’s my hope that by the end of my session you would start to see another way of approaching your work and the creative process all together — while remembering that your process doesn’t have to look like mine, or anyone else’s, but more importantly finding your own voice and way of doing things that feels natural to you. After the boring part, my talk, is over come prepared to have some fun with drawing on paper. There won’t be any prizes or anything like that, just real people drawing real stuff.

Hacking Your Personal Brand Out of the Social Media Swamp
Casey Miller


What’s a personal brand and why do you need one to launch your career? Like it or not, you already have one. And if you aren't actively managing it, your new career may include fries and a large drink. This session will arm you with the basics for finding, monitoring and managing your personal brand -- online and offline. With specific takeaways to complete now, you can grow your personal brand into a powerful tool for long-term career success.

Expected outcomes:
* Learn how to get an accurate snapshot of your current personal brand online
* Use the take-away worksheet to build your personal brand pillars, brand statement and elevator pitch
* Start a flexible plan for how often and where you post, comment and follow online

Let's Talk Photography
Sebron Snyder


Understanding the ever-changing photographic medium will prove to be a valuable asset in your creative career. Whether it involves contracting photography or acquiring images from a commercial image bank, your understanding of the nuances of it's execution and it's potential relevance to your work will allow you to offer more effective creative solutions to your team and clients.

Our open-ended and casual discussion will cover a variety of topics involving commercial, editorial and advertising photography. My goal for this session is to leave you with a better understanding of the integral part photography still plays in all creative endeavors.

A professional photographer now since the early ‘90s, Sebron’s current focus is on editorial and advertising portraiture. He also creates conceptual images, anchored in just the right amount of truth; constantly searching for ways to bend reality in creative and subtle ways. He is known to capture the essence of his subject’s personality and exposes the cleverness and beauty of the profound and the mundane. Sebron has also served as a freelance Art Director and Designer for local creative firms. This experience as a former art director, has allowed Sebron to bring his vast commercial art experience to his present work.

Educator Forum: 2:00 - 3:00p

2015 Industry Expectations: A Conversation about Teaching Visual Communications


In an industry of evolving skillset requirements, how do you keep your curriculum current? As the required competencies and professional options of the graduating student expands, programs are met with the challenges of what to incorporate, where it belongs in the curriculum and how to stay true to teaching idea driven solutions. Good grief. Let’s talk.

Lee Whitmarsh is the Director of Visual Communication for Texas A&M University-Commerce and has logged over twenty years in the classroom. In addition to teaching she directs the cirriculum for 4 areas of emphasis within the BFA in Visual Communication. She introduced and implemented portfolio entry requirements, course rotations, annual student progress assessment and senior exit reviews. She was honored as only the 6th recepient of the Golden Orchard Lifetime Teaching Award by the Dallas Society of Visual Communications.
